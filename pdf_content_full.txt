 
Phase 1: Onboarding & Calibration 
The "Setup" phase ensures the tool knows who you are and what you want to achieve. 
1. LinkedIn Authentication: 
o User installs the extension and clicks "Sign in with LinkedIn." 
o This verifies the account and pulls basic profile data (Name/Headline) to 
save time. 
2. Strategic Goal Setting: 
o Global Context Input: A text area where the user defines their mission. 
o Example: "I'm a Marketing Director at Data Pilot. I'm looking to connect 
with B2B SaaS founders to discuss data automation and partnerships." 
o Daily Outreach Goal: User sets a numeric target (e.g., 20 requests/day). 
The UI provides a "Safety Zone" indicator to help avoid LinkedIn's spam 
filters. 
3. Default Preference Selection: 
o User picks their "Signature Tone" (Casual, Professional, or Direct). 
 
Phase 2: In-Situ Profile Intelligence 
The tool works silently as the user browses LinkedIn profiles. 
1. Passive Detection: 
o As a profile loads, the extension identifies: 
▪ Degree of Connection (1st, 2nd, or 3rd). 
▪ "Open Profile" Status (Is a free InMail possible?). 
▪ The "Stealth" Scrape: Grabs Name, Headline, visible About text, 
and the top Experience entry. 
2. The AI Strategic Badge: 
o When the user opens the extension, the Recommendation Engine 
displays: 
▪ Recommended Channel: "AI Suggests: Connection Request" or 
"AI Suggests: InMail." 
▪ The "Bridge" Reasoning: "You both lead marketing teams in 
Lahore—mentioning local market trends will increase your 
acceptance rate." 
 
Phase 3: Dynamic Drafting & Format Logic 
The UI and AI adapt based on the selected communication channel. 
1. Channel-Specific Interface: 
o Connection Request: One compact text area. Hard-limit logic ensures 
the AI stays under 300 characters. 
o InMail: Two distinct fields: Subject Line (punchy/short) and Body (Macro-
format with a value prop). 
o Message: Conversational, medium-length format for 1st-degree contacts. 
=============
2. The "Bridge" Generation: 
o The AI synthesizes the User's Global Context + Target's Data + 
Selected Tone to write the note. 
o Live Character Counter: Turns red if the text (AI-generated or user-
edited) exceeds LinkedIn's limits. 
 
Phase 4: Execution & Feedback Loop 
Closing the loop and tracking progress. 
1. One-Click Copy: 
o Separate "Copy Subject" and "Copy Message" buttons for InMails. 
o A "Copied!" success toast appears. 
2. Guided Handoff: 
o A small tooltip reminds the user: "Now click 'Add a note' on LinkedIn and 
paste." 
3. Progress Tracking (The "Dopamine Hit"): 
o The Daily Goal Tracker updates instantly: 14 / 20 Goal Reached. 
o A progress bar visually fills up. 
o Success Notification: Once the goal is hit, a "Goal Achieved!" 
celebration appears, encouraging the user to stop to stay within "safe" 
LinkedIn activity limits.
